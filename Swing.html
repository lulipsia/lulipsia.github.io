Swing (Dance)

Group of dances tied to jazz

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Swing dance is a group of social dances that developed with the swing style of jazz music in the 1920sâ€“1940s, with the origins of each dance predating the popular "swing era". Hundreds of styles of swing dancing were developed; those that have survived beyond that era include Charleston, Balboa, Lindy Hop, and Collegiate Shag. Today, the best-known of these dances is the Lindy Hop, which originated in Harlem in the early 1930s . While the majority of swing dances began in African-American communities as vernacular African-American dances , some influenced swing-era dances, like Balboa, developed outside of these communities.
